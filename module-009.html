Fire Protection Equipment Program

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 ⚖ OSHA 29 CFR 1926.24, .150–.155 ⏱ ~15 min read 📝 10-question quiz
🔥1–3. Purpose, Fire Prevention & Water Supply

BBB Tank Services is responsible for developing and maintaining an effective fire protection and prevention program at every job site throughout all phases of construction, repair, alteration, or demolition work. This program applies to all company-controlled work sites where any employee or subcontractor may be exposed to fire hazards.

  • Access to all available firefighting equipment must be maintained at all times.
  • All firefighting equipment must be periodically inspected and maintained in operating condition. Defective equipment must be immediately replaced.
  • A temporary or permanent water supply of sufficient volume, duration, and pressure must be available as soon as combustible materials accumulate on site.
  • A trained and equipped Fire Brigade will be provided on projects where warranted to ensure adequate protection to life.
🧯4. Portable Firefighting Equipment
Daily Inspection Required BBB Tank Services furnishes all portable 20- and 30-pound fire extinguishers for all site locations. These must be charged and inspected daily.
Prohibited Extinguisher Types Carbon tetrachloride and other toxic vaporizing liquid fire extinguishers are prohibited at all BBB Tank Services work locations. All extinguishers must be listed or approved by a nationally recognized testing laboratory.
  • Extinguishers and water drums subject to freezing must be protected from freezing.
  • A 100-foot or less section of 1½-inch hose with a nozzle discharging 25+ gallons per minute may substitute for a 2A-rated extinguisher in the designated area, provided the hose can reach all points in the area.
  • If fire hose connections are not compatible with local firefighting equipment, adapters must be provided.
🧹5. Housekeeping & Compressed Gas Cylinders
  • Remove trash daily from work areas. Use trash drums to reduce extra handling.
  • Rags used for solvent cleaning must be kept in closed metal containers until properly disposed.
  • Paper and other combustible materials may not be allowed to accumulate. Weeds and vegetation must be controlled around tanks, meter stations, and structures.
  • No open burning of trash is allowed at any work location or company premises.

Compressed Gas Cylinders:

20 ft
Oxygen cylinders must be kept at least 20 feet away from fuel cylinders in storage, or separated by a fire-resistant barrier at least 5 feet high with a fire-resistance rating of at least ½ hour.
  • Separate full cylinders from empty cylinders in storage.
  • Keep cylinders tied in a vertical position at all times.
  • Keep oil and grease away from oxygen valves.
  • Turn cylinders off when not in use.
  • Protect cylinders from excess heat — sun, open flames, equipment exhaust, sparks, and slag.
  • No cylinder storage inside buildings.
⛽6. Combustible Material, Engines & Hot Work
15 min
Internal combustion engines must be turned off before refueling and allowed a minimum of 15 minutes to cool before refueling begins.

Material Storage Rules:

  • Storage areas containing combustible material must be separated from other material by at least 20 feet on all sides to prevent fire spread and allow equipment access.
  • A single storage area cannot be larger than 50 × 150 feet.
  • No combustible material may be stored within 10 feet of a building or structure.
  • Material must not be stored within 36 inches of a fire door opening.
  • Roadways and access to storage areas must be maintained to accommodate the widest firefighting vehicle.

Open Flames, Sparks & Hot Work:

  • Check the area for hazards before lighting any torch, match, or heater. Open flames have caused many unnecessary fires.
  • Move flammable or combustible materials before welding or burning. If they can't be moved, cover with fire-retardant material.
  • Tarps must be fire retardant. Plastic sheets must be flame-resistant for use near heat operations.
  • Natural gas and other flammable gases may NOT be used to operate paint sprayers, impact tools, drills, or sandblasting equipment.
  • Personnel working around compressor stations, regulator stations, vaults, or battery rooms where gas or vapor may be present must leave matches, lighters, and smoking material in their vehicle or a remote area.
Temporary Heating Devices Must be located at least 10 feet from combustible tarps, canvas, and plastic coverings. Propane units must have automatic fuel shut-off valves. Oil salamanders must be cool before being refilled or moved. All heaters must be placed level and secured to prevent overturning.
🏭7–10. Flammable & Combustible Liquid Storage & Handling
Definition — Flammable Liquids Any liquid with a flash point below 140°F is classified as a flammable liquid. Handle small quantities (5 gallons maximum) in approved safety cans — which feature a spring-loaded cap and flame arrester. Store in original containers until needed.

Indoor Storage Limits:

Per Cabinet — Flammable
60 gal
Maximum flammable liquids in a single approved storage cabinet
Per Cabinet — Combustible
120 gal
Maximum combustible liquids in a single approved storage cabinet
Max Cabinets Per Building
3
No more than 3 storage cabinets at maximum capacity per single building
Storage Room Aisle
3 ft
Minimum aisle width that must always be maintained inside flammable liquid storage rooms

Outside Storage:

  • No more than 1,100 gallons of flammable or combustible liquids in any one outside storage area unless separated by a minimum 5-foot aisle.
  • Groups of containers must not be nearer than 20 feet to a building.
  • At least one portable fire extinguisher rated not less than 20-B units must be located not less than 25 feet and not more than 75 feet from any outside flammable liquid storage area.

Handling Rules:

  • Dispensing operations must be separated from other operations by not less than 25 feet.
  • When transferring flammable liquids between containers, metallic contact or an electrical bonding jumper must be maintained between the containers to prevent static spark ignition.
  • Use gravity or pump only to draw or transfer flammable liquids — never air pressure on the container or portable tank.
  • Keep flammable liquids in closed containers when not in use.
  • Leakage or spillage must be promptly cleaned up and properly disposed of.
  • Flammable liquids may only be used where there are no open flames or other ignition sources within 50 feet of the operation.
  • Flammable or combustible liquids must NOT be stored in areas used for exits, stairways, or passage of personnel.
🎓11. Training Requirements
  • Employees who may use portable fire extinguishers must receive training on general principles of use, hazards of incipient firefighting, and safe extinguisher use.
  • Training is conducted when the employee is initially assigned and at least annually thereafter.
  • Fire watch personnel must be properly trained before being assigned. Fire watches must be on site prior to beginning hot work and 30 minutes after hot work is complete.
📍12. Fire Extinguisher Mounting & Access
Never Leave on the Floor Extinguishers must NOT be left on the floor, on a scaffold, or on the ground. They must be mounted on a wall, handrail, barricade, or similar fixture.
Over 40 lbs — Max Height
42 in
Top of extinguisher must not exceed 42 inches above the floor
40 lbs or Less — Max Height
5 ft
Top of extinguisher may be mounted as high as 5 feet above the floor

Fire protection equipment must always be properly located and not obstructed from view. Signs must be posted in large rooms to show equipment location. Proper extinguishers must also be carried on company trucks, vans, and specific equipment as needed. Keep trash and stored materials away from extinguishers to prevent blocked access.

🔍13. Extinguisher Inspection & Testing
Visual Inspection
Monthly
Check proper location, fully charged, seal wire not broken, no defects, inspection tag current
Maintenance Check
Annual
Thorough examination by trained individual. Tag showing date of exam, recharge date, and initials attached to each extinguisher
Hydrostatic Test — Most Types
5 yrs
Most extinguisher types require hydrostatic testing every 5 years
Hydrostatic Test — Dry Chemical / Halon
12 yrs
Dry chemical (mild steel), Halon 1211, Halon 1301, and dry powder types require testing every 12 years
6-Year Maintenance Rule Stored-pressure dry chemical extinguishers requiring a 12-year hydrostatic test must be emptied and subjected to maintenance procedures every 6 years. When recharging or hydrostatic testing is performed, the 6-year clock resets from that date.
🚨14. Emergency Action Plan Requirements

All company locations must have a written Emergency Action Plan to ensure employee safety from fire and other emergencies. The plan must include:

  • Emergency escape procedures and emergency route assignments.
  • Procedures for employees who must remain to operate critical operations before evacuating.
  • Procedures to account for all employees after emergency evacuation is complete.
  • Rescue and medical duties for designated employees.
  • Documentation for reporting fires and other emergencies.
  • Names or job titles of personnel who can be contacted for further information.
Posting Requirements Alarm codes and reporting instructions must be conspicuously posted on phones and at employee entrances at all company locations.
