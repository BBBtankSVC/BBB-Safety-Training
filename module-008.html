Fall Protection Equipment Program

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 ⚖ OSHA 29 CFR 1926.500 ⏱ ~18 min read 📝 10-question quiz
📋1. Purpose & Scope

This policy sets requirements and criteria for fall protection at all BBB Tank Services construction workplaces under 29 CFR Part 1926. It applies to all employees and subcontractors working within company-controlled job sites.

Exception This policy does NOT apply when employees are only inspecting, investigating, or assessing workplace conditions prior to the actual start of construction work or after all construction work has been completed.

Falls are the leading cause of death in construction. Fall protection is required whenever employees are potentially exposed to falls from heights of six feet or greater to lower levels, including near excavations. Guardrail systems, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems are the primary methods of protection.

📏3. Critical Height Thresholds — Know These
4 feet
Fall protection required for:
• Leading edges under construction
• Holes in walking/working surfaces
• Ramps, runways, and walkways
• Near dangerous equipment
• Low-slope roofs (unprotected edges)
• Wall openings
• Pre-cast concrete erection
• Hoist areas
6 feet
Fall protection required for:
• All unprotected sides & edges
• Formwork or reinforcing steel
• Steep roofs (unprotected edges)
• Excavation edges (not readily visible)
• Wells, pits, shafts (with guardrail)
• General walking/working surfaces
Dangerous Equipment — Special Rule Employees less than 4 feet above dangerous equipment (tanks, machinery, electrical equipment) must be protected by guardrail systems or equipment guards. At 4 feet or more, all standard fall protection methods apply.
Falling Objects When employees are exposed to falling objects, hard hats are mandatory. The Site Supervisor must also implement toe boards, screens, canopy structures, or barricades to prevent objects from reaching employees below.
🔩4. Guardrail Systems
RequirementSpecification
Top rail height42 inches ± 3 inches above walking/working surface
Mid-rail heightMidway between top rail and walking/working surface
Top rail strengthMust withstand 200 lbs force in any outward or downward direction
Mid-rail strengthMust withstand 150 lbs in any downward or outward direction
Member spacingNo openings wider than 19 inches
Minimum rail sizeAt least ¼ inch diameter or thickness
Wire rope top railMust be flagged every 6 feet with high-visibility material
Prohibited Materials Steel banding and plastic banding may NOT be used as top rails or mid-rails. All surfaces must be smooth to prevent punctures, lacerations, or snagging of clothing.
  • When guardrails are at hoisting areas, a chain, gate, or removable section must be placed across the access opening when hoisting is not taking place.
  • Holes covered by guardrails must have rails on ALL unprotected sides.
  • Safety nets may only be used after approval by the Company Safety Representative.
🦺5. Personal Fall Arrest Systems (PFAS)
ComponentMinimum Strength / Requirement
D-rings and snap hooks5,000 lbs minimum tensile strength
Lanyards & vertical lifelines5,000 lbs minimum breaking strength
Anchorages5,000 lbs per employee attached, or safety factor of at least 2
Self-retracting lifelines (≤2 ft fall)3,000 lbs minimum tensile load in fully extended position
Self-retracting lifelines (>2 ft fall)5,000 lbs minimum tensile load in fully extended position
Horizontal lifelines safety factorAt least 2 — must be supervised by a qualified person
Snap hooks (effective Jan 1, 1998)Only locking type snap hooks permitted
Harness Attachment Point The attachment point of the body harness must be in the center of the wearer's back near shoulder level, or above the wearer's head. The body belt attachment must be in the center of the wearer's back.
Impact Loading — Remove From Service Immediately Any personal fall arrest system or component subjected to impact loading from a fall arrest must be immediately removed from service and must not be used again until inspected and determined undamaged by the Company Safety Representative.
  • PFAS must be inspected prior to each use for wear, damage, and deterioration. Defective components must be removed from service.
  • PFAS must not be attached to guardrail systems, nor to hoists.
  • Harnesses and components are for employee fall protection only — never for hoisting materials.
  • Ropes and straps used in lanyards, lifelines, and body harnesses must be made from synthetic fibers.
  • Snap hooks must not be engaged directly to webbing, rope, to each other, to a D-ring with another snap hook, or to a horizontal lifeline — unless the snap hook is a locking type designed for such connections.
⚓6. Positioning Device Systems
2 ft
Positioning devices must be rigged so an employee cannot free fall more than 2 feet.
  • Positioning devices must be secured to an anchorage capable of supporting at least twice the potential impact load of an employee's fall, or 3,000 lbs — whichever is greater.
  • Positioning device systems must be inspected prior to each use for wear, damage, and deterioration. Defective components must be removed from service.
🚧8. Controlled Access Zones & Warning Line Systems

Controlled access zones (CAZs) may only be used with approval from the Company Safety Representative. When control lines are used:

  • Control lines must be erected not less than 6 feet and not more than 25 feet from the unprotected or leading edge.
  • Lines must run along the entire length of the unprotected edge and be parallel to it.
  • Lines must be flagged or marked at not more than 6-foot intervals with high-visibility material.
  • Lowest point of line (including sag) must be at least 39 inches from the walking/working surface; highest point no more than 45 inches.
  • Each line must have a minimum breaking strength of 200 lbs.
Company Safety Representative Approval Required For: Safety nets · Warning line systems · Controlled access zones · Safety monitoring systems
🔲10. Covers for Holes
  • Vehicle-area covers must support at least twice the maximum axle load of the largest vehicle expected to cross.
  • All other covers must support at least twice the weight of employees, equipment, and materials that may be imposed at any one time.
  • All covers must be secured when installed to prevent accidental displacement by wind, equipment, or employees.
Covers Must Be Marked All covers must be color coded or marked with the word "HOLE" or "COVER" to provide warning of the hazard.
👁9. Safety Monitoring Systems

When no other alternative fall protection measure is feasible, a safety monitoring system may be implemented — but only with Company Safety Representative approval. The designated safety monitor must:

  • Be competent to recognize fall hazards.
  • Warn employees when they are unaware of a fall hazard or are acting unsafely.
  • Be on the same walking/working surface and within visual sighting distance of employees being monitored.
  • Be close enough for oral communication.
  • Have no other responsibilities that could take attention away from the monitoring function.
🎓11–13. Training, Rescue & Accident Investigation

The company provides fall hazard training for every employee who may be exposed to fall hazards. Training must cover:

  • The nature of fall hazards in the work area.
  • Correct procedures for erecting, maintaining, disassembling, and inspecting fall protection systems.
  • The use and operation of guardrail systems, PFAS, safety nets, warning line systems, safety monitoring systems, and controlled access zones.
  • Correct procedures for handling and storage of equipment and materials.
  • The employee's role in fall protection plans and safety monitoring systems.
Training Documentation Training must be documented with written certification records showing the employee's name, date(s) of training, and the trainer's signature. Retraining is required when deficiencies are noted, procedures change, or equipment modifications make prior training obsolete.
Rescue Planning The Site Supervisor must develop a site-specific rescue plan for employees who fall. This plan must include procedures allowing employees to rescue themselves when able. After any fall or near-miss, an accident investigation must be conducted to determine if the fall protection plan needs revision.
