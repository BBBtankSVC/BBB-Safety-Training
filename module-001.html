Accident Prevention Plan

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 🏢 BBB Tank Services, LLC ⏱ ~15 min read 📝 10-question quiz
📋 1. Statement of Company Safety Policy
Core Commitment BBB Tank Services LLC is committed to the continuous improvement of environmental, health, and safety to achieve the greatest benefit for all employees, clients, and the environment.

It is company policy to meet or exceed all applicable environmental, health, and safety laws and regulations. The Environmental, Health and Safety Policy is a standard by which all employees are continually measured.

Our three goal areas under this policy:

⚙ Operations
Integrate EHS stewardship into all core business activities and planning. Establish total employee involvement in EHS processes.
📢 Communications
Promote EHS awareness with clients and communities. Cooperate with local, state, and federal authorities on safety matters.
📊 Evaluation
Incorporate EHS performance metrics into management reporting. Conduct independent compliance audits and investigate incidents and near misses.
Remember Every employee is responsible for compliance with this policy and for implementing it within their area of responsibility.
🏛 2. Authority & Accountability

The CEO is ultimately responsible for providing resources and guidance for the Safety & Health Program, and for selecting the Company Director of Safety.

The Director of Safety handles day-to-day implementation of the working plan. The CEO retains final responsibility even when portions are delegated.

Supervisors have the duty and authority to carry out disciplinary actions for safety violations.

Contractors Contractors working on company property must comply with BBB safety standards AND federal OSHA requirements. Their safety programs will be reviewed before work begins and must meet OSHA requirements and TRIR limits.
👷 3–5. Roles & Responsibilities

Director of Safety

  • Conduct safety inspections, surveys, audits, and assessments throughout the workplace.
  • Maintain appropriate training and testing records for each employee.
  • Investigate accidents, injuries, illnesses, and near-miss incidents.
  • Ensure reportable injuries are documented on OSHA forms 300A, 301, and 300.
  • Monitor and report directly to the CEO on safety program results.

Supervisors

  • Ensure compliance with safety rules through daily supervision of employees.
  • Audit daily JSAs for initials or updates.
  • Orient and mentor new hires, temp employees, and reassigned employees on safe work practices before they begin work.
  • Report and investigate accidents and near-miss incidents.
  • Immediately notify management (operations and safety director) when a near-miss or accident occurs.
⚠ Priority Rule for Supervisors If a hazard presents immediate danger to life or health, the Supervisor must intervene immediately to protect persons — this is the primary priority. Preventing property damage is secondary.

Employees

  • Learn, understand, and follow all safety rules and safe work procedures.
  • Utilize PPE when required.
  • Report observed hazardous conditions immediately to their supervisor.
  • If a supervisor is not immediately available, act to protect others from the hazard (e.g., take a malfunctioning tool out of service), then notify a supervisor at the earliest opportunity — no later than end of shift.
✓ Your Right as an Employee Employees are NOT required to perform any task they believe to be dangerous or unsafe. Suspend the task and discuss with your supervisor. Safety compliance is a condition of employment.
🤝 6. Safety Meetings

Safety meetings are conducted periodically with all employees and supervisors. Their primary function is to promote safety awareness and communication throughout the workplace.

  • Conducted by the site Safety Representative, Supervisor, or designee.
  • Include ongoing safety training and discussions of current safety issues.
  • A written attendance record, signed by each employee, is maintained as documentation for every meeting.
  • If a scheduled meeting must be postponed, it will be rescheduled by the Safety Representative.
🛡 7. Employees Reporting a Hazard Are Protected
Non-Retaliation Policy No person will discharge or discriminate against any employee who reports a known or perceived safety hazard to management or any governmental authority. This protection is absolute.

Employees who believe they have been discriminated against for reporting a hazard should report this directly to the Company Safety Manager or an appointed alternate.

False claims filed primarily to harass the Company or individuals will result in appropriate action by management.

🔍 9. Safety & Health Self-Inspections
Daily
A Job Safety Analysis (JSA) must be performed every day, prior to commencing each job.

The site Supervisor is responsible for completing the Daily JSA. All on-site employees are required to sign the JSA, indicating their understanding of the analysis. Each JSA must include the date, job site location, and description.

Monthly
Supervisors in each department and job site conduct scheduled "in-house" safety and health self-inspections at least monthly in their area of responsibility.

Inspections cover tools, equipment, machinery, operating procedures, and any existing or potential hazards. All observations are tracked through OMNIDEK.

What If You Find a Hazard During Inspection? Hazards that can be corrected easily are corrected immediately. Work will not proceed until all hazards are mitigated. Employees will be notified of any hazard that poses an immediate threat.
🚨 10. Accident Reporting & Investigation
Report Without Delay All employees must report the following to their immediate Supervisor as quickly as possible and without delay:
  • Accidents or incidents resulting in injury or illness of any magnitude (including first aid cases)
  • Accidents resulting in significant property or equipment damage
  • Any near-miss incidents that could potentially have resulted in injury or property damage

Accident Investigation is conducted by the site Supervisor and Safety Representative. They determine:

  • How the accident or incident occurred
  • Special circumstances involved
  • Underlying, indirect, or associated causes
  • Corrective actions and preventive measures
Immediate Notification Management (operations and safety director) must be notified immediately when a near-miss or accident occurs.
🎓 11–13. Orientation & Training Requirements

BBB Tank Services provides initial safety and health orientation and ongoing training to all employees at every level.

New Hire Requirement New Hire Safety Orientation Training is administered to all new employees prior to their initial work assignment. No new employee begins work without completing safety orientation.

Training covers:

  • Potential hazards associated with the work area and specific job assignments
  • Emergency procedures
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) requirements
  • Hazard Communication (Right-to-Know) for chemicals used in the workplace
  • Specific equipment operations training related to employee tasks

Three types of safety training are used:

1. New Hire Orientation
Completed before initial work assignment for all new employees.
2. Reassigned Personnel Orientation
For employees who change job roles or job sites.
3. Ongoing / Annual Training
Continuous safety education for all employees throughout employment.
🗄 8 & 14. Record Keeping & Medical Record Access
30 yrs
Employee medical and exposure records must be preserved for the duration of employment plus 30 years, per OSHA Standard 29 CFR 1910.1020.

Employees have direct access to their own exposure and medical records. Requests are made in writing, and access must be provided within 15 days. All records are maintained at:

Records Location BBB Tank Services LLC · Attn: Recordkeeping · 162 Independence Pkwy N · Baytown, TX 77520

OSHA authorized representatives also have the right to examine and copy these records. An effective recordkeeping program is the only valid way to review and identify trends and deficiencies in the safety program.

📅 15. Annual Review & Program Revision

At least yearly, the Director of Safety, management, and other designated personnel review all components of the Accident Prevention Plan and Safety & Health Program.

  • New procedures, processes, and equipment introductions are given special attention.
  • OSHA logs are kept up and reported quarterly with reportable accidents and illnesses.
  • BBB Tank Services submits OSHA 300A Summary and OSHA 301 forms by the March 2nd deadline of the following year.
