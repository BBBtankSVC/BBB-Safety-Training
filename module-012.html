Forklift Safety Program

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 ⚖ OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 ⏱ ~15 min read 📝 10-question quiz
📋1–2. Purpose & General Procedures

This policy covers safety requirements for the design, maintenance, and use of fork trucks, tractors, platform lift trucks, motorized hand trucks, and other specialized industrial trucks powered by electric motors or internal combustion engines at all BBB Tank Services-controlled job sites.

Authorized Operators Only Only trained, authorized, and BBB Tank Services certified operators are permitted to operate powered industrial trucks. No employee may operate a forklift before completing the full training and evaluation program.

Key general rules:

  • No modifications affecting capacity or safe operation without manufacturer's prior written approval.
  • No person may stand or pass under the elevated portion of any truck — whether loaded or empty.
  • No unauthorized personnel may ride on powered industrial trucks.
  • Do not place arms or legs between the uprights of the mast or outside the running lines of the truck.
  • Fork trucks may not be driven up to anyone standing in front of a bench or other fixed object.
  • Where general lighting is less than 2 lumens per square foot, auxiliary directional lighting must be provided on the truck.
Leaving the Truck — What Must Be Done When a powered industrial truck is left unattended: load engaging means fully lowered · controls neutralized · power shut off · brakes set · wheels blocked if on an incline. When dismounted but within 25 feet and the truck is still in view: load fully lowered · controls neutralized · brakes set.
25 ft
A truck is considered "unattended" when the operator is 25 feet or more away from the vehicle while it remains in view — OR whenever the operator leaves the vehicle and it is not in view.
🚜3. Traveling Rules
3 lengths
Maintain a safe following distance of approximately three truck lengths from the truck ahead. Always keep the truck under control.
  • Observe all traffic regulations including authorized plant speed limits.
  • Yield the right of way to ambulances, fire trucks, and emergency vehicles.
  • Do not pass other trucks traveling in the same direction at intersections, blind spots, or dangerous locations.
  • Slow down and sound the horn at cross aisles and other locations where vision is obstructed.
  • If the load obstructs the forward view, travel with the load trailing.
  • Look in the direction of travel and maintain a clear view of the path at all times.
  • Stunt driving and horseplay are prohibited.
  • Slow down for wet and slippery floors. Avoid running over loose objects.
  • Cross railroad tracks diagonally wherever possible. Never park closer than 8 feet from the center of railroad tracks.
Grades Greater Than 10% When ascending or descending grades of more than 10%, loaded trucks must be driven with the load upgrade (facing uphill). On all grades, the load and forks must be tilted back and raised only as far as necessary to clear the road surface.
📦4. Loading Rules
  • Only stable or safely arranged loads may be handled. Use caution with off-center loads that cannot be centered.
  • Never exceed the truck's rated capacity.
  • Trucks equipped with attachments must be operated as partially loaded when not managing a load.
  • Tilting the load forward with the load engaging means elevated is prohibited — except to pick up a load or deposit it over a rack or stack.
  • When stacking or tiering, use only enough backward tilt to stabilize the load.
  • Long or high loads that may affect capacity must be adjusted accordingly.
🔧5–6. Operation & Maintenance
🔴 Never Do These
  • Fuel truck while engine is running
  • Use open flame to check electrolyte level in batteries or gasoline level in fuel tanks
  • Operate with a fuel system leak until corrected
  • Use low flash point solvents (below 100°F) for cleaning
  • Alter truck without manufacturer approval
  • Add counterweighting without manufacturer approval
✅ Always Do These
  • Remove unsafe truck from service immediately
  • Have authorized personnel perform all repairs
  • Disconnect battery before electrical repairs
  • Conduct fuel/ignition repairs only in designated locations
  • Use only safety-equivalent replacement parts
  • Keep truck clean — free of lint, excess oil, and grease
Daily
Industrial trucks must be examined before each use. For round-the-clock operations, examination is required after each shift. Any defect found must be immediately reported and corrected.

If any part of the truck is found to be operating above normal temperature, creating a hazardous condition, the vehicle must be removed from service until the cause of overheating is eliminated.

Fuel spillage must be carefully washed away or completely evaporated before restarting the engine. The fuel tank cap must be replaced.

🎓7. Training & Recertification Requirements

All forklift operators must be trained before initial assignment and at least annually thereafter. Training combines formal instruction, practical demonstrations, and workplace performance evaluation.

Refresher Training Required When:
  • The operator is observed operating unsafely or is involved in a near-miss incident.
  • The operator is assigned to drive a different type of truck.
  • Workplace conditions change in a way that could affect safe operation.
3 years
An evaluation and recertification of each operator's performance must be conducted at least once every three years.

The Company certifies each operator's training in a written record that includes:

  • Name of the operator
  • Date of the training
  • Date of the evaluation
  • Identity of the person(s) who performed the training or evaluation
Training Topics Include: Truck controls and instrumentation · Operating instructions and warnings · Differences between truck and automobile · Vehicle capacity and stability · Refueling and battery charging · Surface conditions · Load stability · Pedestrian traffic awareness · Ramps and sloped surfaces · Hazardous locations · Carbon monoxide/exhaust buildup in enclosed environments
