Confined Space Entry Program

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 ⚖ OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146 & 1926.21(b)(6) ⏱ ~20 min read 📝 10-question quiz
📋1. Purpose & Scope

This program establishes requirements for protecting BBB Tank Services employees and subcontractors from the hazards associated with entry into permit-required confined spaces. It applies to all employees and subcontractors working within company-controlled worksites.

Why Confined Spaces Are Deadly Confined spaces can contain hazardous atmospheres, engulfment hazards, internal configuration hazards, and other recognized serious safety and health hazards. Many confined space fatalities occur when would-be rescuers enter without proper training or equipment. This program exists to prevent those deaths.
⚠ DANGER — PERMIT-REQUIRED CONFINED SPACE — DO NOT ENTER

This sign (or similar language) must be posted wherever permit-required confined spaces exist at any BBB Tank Services worksite.

📖2. Key Definitions
Confined Space
Large enough to enter; has limited/restricted means for entry or exit; is NOT designed for continuous employee occupancy; or has inadequate ventilation.
Entry
Occurs as soon as any part of the entrant's body breaks the plane of an opening into a permit space. All ensuing work activities inside are also considered "entry."
Attendant
An individual stationed outside a permit-required confined space who monitors authorized entrants and performs all assigned attendant duties.
Authorized Entrant
An employee specifically authorized by the Company to enter a permit space.
Entry Supervisor
The person responsible for determining if acceptable entry conditions are present, authorizing entry, overseeing operations, and terminating entry as required.
Isolation
The process of completely protecting a permit space against release of energy or material — by blanking/blinding, lockout/tagout, double block and bleed, or disconnecting mechanical linkages.
Blanking / Blinding
The absolute closure of a pipe, line, or duct by fastening a solid plate capable of withstanding maximum pressure with no leakage.
Competent Person
One capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards AND who has the authority to take prompt corrective measures to eliminate them.
📝3. General Requirements
  • The Site Supervisor evaluates the workplace to determine if any permit-required confined spaces exist. The client may provide a list at remote locations.
  • Employees must be informed of the existence, location, and dangers of permit spaces — by posted danger signs or equally effective means.
  • If employees will NOT enter a permit space, measures will be taken to prevent all entry.
  • If employees WILL enter, a site-specific written permit space program must be developed and implemented before entry begins.
  • When entrance covers are removed, the opening must be immediately guarded by a railing, temporary cover, or barrier to prevent accidental falls and protect employees from falling objects.
Program Review Triggers The permit space program must be reviewed after: any unauthorized entry, detection of a hazard not covered by the permit, a prohibited condition being detected, any injury or near-miss during entry, any change in use or configuration of the space, or employee complaints about the program.
📄4. Site-Specific Written Program

Before entry into any confined space at any BBB Tank Services worksite, a site-specific written program must be developed by the Site Supervisor with input from the Safety Director. It must comply with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146 and include:

  • Measures to prevent unauthorized entry.
  • Methods to identify and evaluate hazards of permit spaces before entry.
  • Acceptable entry conditions and methods to verify they are maintained throughout entry.
  • Methods used to isolate the permit space (lockout/tagout, blanking, blinding, etc.).
  • Purging, flushing, or ventilating procedures to eliminate atmospheric hazards.
  • Identification of authorized entrants, attendants, and entry supervisors.
  • Testing and monitoring equipment needed, ventilating equipment, communications equipment, PPE, and lighting.
  • Equipment for safe ingress and egress (ladders, etc.).
  • Rescue and emergency services provided and training required.
🚪5. Entry Procedure Guidelines
Mandatory — No Exceptions All entrants must wear a safety harness with retrieval rope attached to the D-ring on the BACK of the harness at all times during confined space entry.

Before any employee enters a confined space, the internal atmosphere must be tested in this exact order:

  1. Oxygen content — must be within acceptable range (19.5%–23.5%)
  2. Flammable gases and vapors — must be below lower explosive limit
  3. Potential toxic air contaminants — must be below permissible exposure limits
Testing Must Use Calibrated Instruments Atmospheric testing must be performed with a calibrated direct-reading instrument. The entry supervisor must notify all entrants of hazards and monitoring results. Entrants will review and sign the written permit.

During Entry — Critical Rules:

  • No hazardous atmosphere may exist within the space while any employee is inside.
  • Forced air ventilation must be used to eliminate hazardous atmospheres before entry and must continue until all employees have left the space.
  • The atmosphere must be continuously monitored throughout the entry to ensure ventilation is working and oxygen levels are adequate.
  • If a hazardous atmosphere is detected during entry, all employees must leave the space immediately. The space must be evaluated and hazards mitigated before any re-entry.
  • All entrants must be signed in and out by the attendant every time they enter or exit.
  • The duration of the permit may not exceed the time required to complete the assigned task.
Lockout/Tagout Required Lockout/Tagout procedures must be followed during all permit-required confined space entries.

Entry Permit: The entry supervisor signs the permit before entry begins. The completed permit is posted at the entry portal (or equally visible location) so all entrants can confirm pre-entry preparations are complete. Entry is terminated and the permit cancelled when operations are complete, a prohibited condition arises, or the work area emergency system is activated.

At least one attendant must be stationed outside the permit space for the entire duration of entry operations. If one attendant monitors multiple spaces, the program must include procedures to allow response to any space emergency without compromising duties at other spaces.

👷6. Roles & Duties of Each Position

Authorized Entrants must:

  • Know the hazards that may be faced during entry — mode, signs, symptoms, and consequences of exposure.
  • Properly use all equipment as required by the permit.
  • Maintain communication with the attendant throughout entry.
  • Alert the attendant immediately when they recognize any warning sign, symptom of exposure, or a prohibited condition.
  • Exit the space as quickly as possible when ordered by the attendant or entry supervisor, when they recognize any warning sign, or when an evacuation alarm is activated.

Attendants must:

  • Know all hazards that may be faced, including behavioral effects of hazard exposure in entrants.
  • Continuously maintain an accurate count of all authorized entrants inside the space.
  • Remain outside the permit space at all times during entry operations until relieved by another attendant.
  • Communicate with entrants continuously to monitor their status.
  • Order immediate evacuation if a prohibited condition is detected, behavioral effects of hazard exposure are observed in an entrant, an outside situation arises that could endanger entrants, or if the attendant cannot safely perform all duties.
  • Summon rescue and emergency services immediately when entrants may need assistance.
  • Warn unauthorized persons away from the permit space and advise them to exit immediately if they have entered.
  • Perform no duties that might interfere with the primary duty of monitoring and protecting authorized entrants.
⚠ Attendant Rescue Rule — Critical If an entrant needs rescue, the attendant's sole responsibility is to sound the alarm to evacuate other entrants and summon emergency personnel. Under NO circumstance will an attendant enter the confined space by themselves.

Entry Supervisors must:

  • Know all hazards and verify that all tests, procedures, and equipment required by the permit are in place before authorizing entry.
  • Verify that rescue services are available and that means of summoning them are operable.
  • Terminate entry and cancel the permit when required.
  • Remove unauthorized individuals who enter or attempt to enter the permit space.
  • Verify at intervals that entry conditions remain consistent with the terms of the entry permit.
🚨7. Emergency Response & Rescue
  • The Site Supervisor must confirm in advance that rescue and emergency services are available for IDLH situations, with phone numbers posted at the work location.
  • A reliable communication method (phone, cell, two-way radio) must be available at all times to summon emergency services.
  • If emergency services cannot respond in a timely manner, competent confined space rescue personnel must stand by at the work location during all confined space operations.
  • Dedicated rescue personnel must be trained, properly equipped, and authorized by the Operations Manager and Safety Director. Unauthorized personnel are prohibited from attempting a rescue.
  • Third-party rescue services must be allowed to survey the work location, select specialized equipment, and may decline the standby assignment.
  • All PPE for rescue personnel is provided by the Company at no cost.
Annual
Authorized BBB rescue personnel must receive training and hands-on practice rescues annually to maintain proficiency.
