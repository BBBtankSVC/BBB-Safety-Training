Aerial Lift Safety Program

📅 Rev. 10/31/2025 ⚖ OSHA 29 CFR 1926.453 & 1910.67 ⏱ ~15 min read 📝 10-question quiz
📋1 & 2. Purpose & Scope
Purpose This program establishes the minimum requirements for the safe operation of aerial lifts by BBB Tank Services personnel and contractors. No employee may operate an aerial lift without completing training, certification, and authorization first.

This program applies to all company and contractor personnel who operate aerial lifts at any BBB Tank Services work location. Each new or transfer-affected employee required to operate aerial lifts must be trained, certified, and authorized before operating any equipment.

The goal is to prevent accidents, injuries, and property damage caused by the unsafe operation of aerial lift equipment.

👷3. Responsibilities
Site Supervisor
Has ultimate authority. Responsible for ensuring this Aerial Lift Safety Program is fully implemented at the job site.
Department Supervisors
Responsible for initial proficiency training, authorizing qualified individuals, and implementing all policies in their area.
Safety Representative
Responsible for providing or obtaining safety training to designated personnel. Conducts classroom instruction and written testing.
Operators
Must demonstrate competency through training and experience. Primary responsibility is to use aerial lifts safely at all times.
⚙4. Aerial Lift Specifications

All aerial lifts acquired for use on or after January 22, 1973 must be designed and constructed in accordance with ANSI A92.2-1969 standards for Vehicle Mounted Elevating and Rotating Work Platforms.

Aerial lifts acquired before January 22, 1973 that do not meet these requirements will not be used unless they have been modified to conform to ANSI A92.2-1969 requirements.

Obstructed Rear View When an aerial lift has an obstructed view to the rear, the vehicle must have either a reverse signal alarm audible above surrounding noise, or an observer to signal the operator when it is safe to back up.
🎓5. Safety Training Requirements
Mandatory — No Exceptions Only trained and authorized operators are permitted to operate aerial lifts. This is non-negotiable.

Safety training must cover all of the following:

  • Federal OSHA regulations in 29 CFR 1910.67 (Vehicle-mounted elevating and rotating work platforms)
  • Basic principles and safe work procedures for aerial lifts
  • Pre-work operator inspection procedures
  • Safe work procedures for authorized personnel
  • Safe operations near other personnel, pedestrians, electrical sources, and obstructions
  • Recognizing hazards specific to aerial lift operations
Written Test Required Each participant will receive written materials and complete a written test immediately following classroom instruction. Participants must pass this test at a score acceptable to the EH&S Office to receive certification.
🏅6 & 7. Certification Process & Badge System

Certification is a three-step process:

Step 1 — Basic Competency
Determined by the department supervisor after sufficient direct observation of the operator's ability to operate the equipment safely.
Step 2 — Classroom Instruction
Safety Trainer presents instruction and gives a written test. Trainer issues written certification to participants who pass.
Step 3 — Direct Observation
Conducted by the PIT Crew on a department-by-department basis. Department supervisor must concur with each certification.
Certification Badge Operators who complete all three steps are issued a color-coded badge that must be worn or carried on their person when operating aerial lift equipment at any company workplace.
✅8. Qualifications of Aerial Lift Operators
  • Only trained and authorized persons may operate aerial lifts.
  • Operators must be qualified as to visual, auditory, physical, and mental ability to operate the equipment safely.
  • Only employees determined by the supervisor to be competent by reason of training and experience may operate aerial lifts.
  • Employees being trained under direct supervision may operate equipment during the training period only.
Medical Disqualification No employee known to have defective uncorrected eyesight or hearing, or suffering from heart disease, epilepsy, or other ailments that may suddenly incapacitate them, will be permitted to operate an aerial lift.
Unsafe Operation Warning Unsafe or improper operation of an aerial lift can result in death or serious injury to the operator or others, and damage to product, facilities, or other property.
🏗9. General Requirements — Aerial Boom Lifts
Daily
Controls must be checked and tested each day prior to use to confirm they are in safe operating condition.
  • All personnel in the platform must wear an approved safety harness with lanyard attached to the platform attachment point.
  • Never exceed the manufacturer's specified load limits for booms and baskets.
  • Aerial lifts may only be used as intended by the manufacturer — no field modifications unless certified by the manufacturer.
  • A malfunctioning lift must be shut down until repaired.
  • Instruction on warning placards must be legible at all times.
  • Personnel must always stand on the floor of the platform — never on boxes, planks, or railings.
  • Ground controls will not be operated unless permission is obtained from personnel in the platform, except in an emergency.
  • Before raising or extending the boom, the machine must be positioned on a firm, level, and uniform supporting surface.
10 ft
Minimum clearance distance between any part of the aerial lift (or its load) and any electrical line or apparatus carrying up to 50,000 volts (50 kV). Add 1 foot for every additional 30,000 volts.
🚗10. Driving & Towing Rules
  • Always post a lookout when driving in areas where vision is restricted.
  • Machine must be in a stowed position and turntable secured when towing.
  • Always position boom in line with the direction of travel.
  • Always secure turntable prior to any extended traveling.
  • Never permit personnel on the machine or platform when towing.
  • Machine must be mechanically assisted when traveling grades exceeding 15 degrees.
  • Do not travel on soft or uneven surfaces — tipping can occur.
⚠ No Highway Towing Towing is permitted only for emergency travel on the job site. No highway towing permitted.
⚠11. Critical Operation Rules
  • This machine is NOT electronically insulated — always maintain clearance from electrical lines.
  • Never use the boom for any purpose other than positioning personnel and their tools and equipment.
  • Check clearances above, on sides, and below the platform when raising, lowering, swinging, and telescoping the boom.
  • Keep mud, oil, grease, and slippery substances off footwear and platform decks.
  • Enter and exit through the gate only.
  • Never exceed the manufacturer's rated platform capacity.
  • Do not allow ground personnel in areas around and under a raised platform.
  • Approved hard hats must be worn by all operating and ground personnel.
  • Always secure the safety harness lanyard to the platform attachment point — NEVER to an adjacent object or structure.
  • Never attempt to free the machine by lifting it off the ground with the boom.
  • Stow the boom and shut off all power before leaving the machine.
100% Tie-Off Required Never exit the aerial lift without being properly tied off to an adjacent structure with a 5,000 lb anchor point. 100% tie-off is always required while entering or exiting the aerial lift.
🔧12. Scissor Lift — Pre-Start Inspection

Conduct a walk-around inspection at the beginning of each shift prior to using a scissor lift. This inspection must include:

1.Check scissor end joints for cracks and broken weld points.
2.Check hydraulic cylinders, hoses, and fittings.
3.Check drive axles, wheels, and hubs for loose, damaged, or missing parts.
4.Check tires for cracks, cuts, bulges, and proper inflation.
5.Check battery cables for fraying or broken insulation; verify terminal posts are not loose.
6.Check for corrosion and cracking around battery terminals.
7.On propane or diesel lifts, check the fuel tank for cracks, broken welds, or damage — always outdoors, never indoors.
8.Make sure a fire extinguisher is on hand.
9.Check outriggers, stabilizers, and guardrails.
10.Read the rating capacity and placard.
Out of Service Lifts After conducting the walk-around, elevate the lift from ground controls as a functional test. Any lift out of service for 3 months or longer must be inspected by a qualified mechanic before use.
🦺13 & 14. Scissor Lift Training & Fall Protection

All new employees whose job requires operating a scissor lift must be trained before operation. Operators must:

  • Read the manufacturer's operating manual prior to first-time operation.
  • Understand all factory control functions before being authorized to operate.
  • Inspect the work area for hazards: uneven surfaces, debris, and overhead obstructions or electrical lines.
  • Keep 10–35 feet between the lift and any power lines. Add more distance for swaying lines and platform movement.

Fall protection rules for scissor lifts:

  • Guard rails must be in place at all times.
  • You must be tied off to the lift, not to an adjacent structure.
  • Keep pedestrians at least 6 feet away from the lift.
  • Never drive the lift on a slope exceeding the grade on the placard.
  • Never stand on boxes, ladders, planks, or railings on the platform.
  • Platform must be within one foot of an adjacent structure before attempting to enter it.
  • Never leave cords from power tools hanging off the platform.
  • Before raising or lowering, make sure other employees on the lift are aware the platform is about to move.
  • Retract stabilizers and leveling jacks prior to moving the lift.
